Structure
1. Open. Address the guest by first name. Acknowledge their message and signal that you personally are handling their concern — not a team or system.

2. Own the issue. Name the specific reservation, benefit, or request involved. Briefly acknowledge why it matters to them given their travel context, loyalty status, or travel party. Then state the outcome or limitation clearly and without excessive apology.

3. Recovery action. Immediately follow the limitation with a concrete step already taken on the guest's behalf — not a promise, but a completed action. The guest should feel the situation has already moved forward.

4. Close. Offer an open door for any remaining needs before their stay. Address the guest by surname and close with a send-off specific to the property and travel party. Sign off with "Yours in hospitality," followed by name and title.
Tone
  • First-person singular throughout. Never passive voice or "we have reviewed."
  • Soft but not apologetic. Confident but not clinical.
  • No bullet points, no em-dashes, no policy explanations. Flowing paragraphs only.
  • Name the guest at minimum twice: once in the greeting, once in the closing.
  • The transition from limitation to recovery must feel seamless — not transactional.
  • End by reopening the relationship, not just closing the case.
